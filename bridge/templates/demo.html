What is a Bridge?

A bridge is a structure built to span a physical obstacle — such as a body of water, valley, road, or rail — without blocking the way underneath. Bridges are among the most critical infrastructure elements in modern civilization.

Types of Bridges

Beam Bridge

Simplest type. Horizontal beam supported at each end.

Arch Bridge

Curved structure distributing weight outward along the curve.

Suspension Bridge

Deck hangs from cables anchored at each end and draped over towers.

Truss Bridge

Uses triangular units to distribute loads efficiently.

Cantilever Bridge

Built using cantilevers — structures anchored at one end only.

Cable-Stayed

Cables run directly from towers to the deck in a fan pattern.

Why Maintenance Matters

Structural Fatigue

Repeated stress cycles cause micro-cracks. Without detection and repair, these grow into catastrophic failures.

Corrosion

Steel reinforcement corrodes when exposed to moisture and chemicals, weakening the structure from within.

Overloading

Bridges designed for certain loads may face heavier modern traffic, accelerating wear and tear.

Weather Damage

Temperature swings, floods, and high winds cause expansion joints to fail and foundations to erode.
